1.1 Slopes and Equations of Lines
Objective: Find slopes, write linear equations in three forms, and identify parallel and perpendicular lines.
slope horizontal & vertical three forms parallel perpendicular
Concept
Slope
definition
\[\text{Slope} = m = \dfrac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}} = \dfrac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}\]
Positive slope
Line climbs as \(x\) increases — both steps point the same direction.
Negative slope
Line falls as \(x\) increases — the vertical step flips sign.
Reading the formula
Slope measures change in \(y\) per unit change in \(x\). Pick any two points on the line — the ratio is constant for every pair.

Example 1
Line with an Undefined Slope
A line passes through the point \((3,\,5)\) and has an undefined slope. Write the equation of the line.
Step 1 · Identify the line type
Undefined slope \(\Rightarrow\) the line is vertical.
Step 2 · Use the vertical-line form
\[x = k\]
Vertical lines always look like \(x = \text{constant}\) — the \(x\)-coordinate never changes.
Answer
\[x = 3\]
Reference
Three Forms of a Linear Equation
slope-intercept form
\[y = mx + b\]
point-slope form
\[y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)\]
standard form
\[Ax + By = C\]
  • Slope-intercept is best when you know the slope and \(y\)-intercept.
  • Point-slope is best when you know a slope and any point on the line.
  • Standard form uses integer coefficients with \(A \ge 0\) — useful for systems and graphing by intercepts.
Example 2
Write the Equation in All Three Forms
Find an equation of the line that passes through \((1,\,-2)\) and has slope \(3\). Write your answer in all three forms.
Step 1 · Substitute into point-slope form
\[y - (-2) = 3(x - 1)\]
Plug \(m = 3\), \(x_1 = 1\), \(y_1 = -2\) into \(y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)\).
Point-slope form
\[y + 2 = 3(x - 1)\]
Slope-intercept form
\[y = 3x - 5\]
Distribute \(3\) and subtract \(2\) from both sides.
Standard form
\[3x - y = 5\]
Move \(3x\) to the left and multiply by \(-1\) so the leading coefficient is positive.
Example 3
Subscriber Growth — Real-World Linear Model
An influencer had 120,000 subscribers in 2022 and 182,500 in 2024. Let \(x\) = years since 2020 and \(y\) = subscribers. Write a linear equation for \(y\) in terms of \(x\), then predict her subscribers in 2027.
Step 1 · Translate to coordinate points
\[(2,\,120{,}000) \quad\text{and}\quad (4,\,182{,}500)\]
2022 is 2 years after 2020; 2024 is 4 years after 2020.
Step 2 · Find the slope
\[m = \dfrac{182{,}500 - 120{,}000}{4 - 2} = \dfrac{62{,}500}{2} = 31{,}250\]
Step 3 · Point-slope form
\[y - 120{,}000 = 31{,}250(x - 2)\]
Step 4 · Slope-intercept form
\[y = 31{,}250x + 57{,}500\]
Distribute and add \(120{,}000\) to both sides.
Step 5 · Predict 2027 \((x = 7)\)
\[y = 31{,}250(7) + 57{,}500 = 276{,}250 \text{ subscribers}\]

Example 4
Equation of a Perpendicular Line
Find the slope-intercept form of the line that passes through \((2,\,-1)\) and is perpendicular to \(2x - 3y = 5\).
Step 1 · Rewrite the given line in slope-intercept form
\[2x - 3y = 5 \;\Longrightarrow\; y = \dfrac{2}{3}x - \dfrac{5}{3}\]
Solve for \(y\). Subtract \(2x\), then divide by \(-3\).
Step 2 · Identify the given slope
\[m_{\text{given}} = \dfrac{2}{3}\]
Step 3 · Take the opposite reciprocal
\[m_{\perp} = -\dfrac{3}{2}\]
Flip the fraction and change the sign.
Step 4 · Point-slope form
\[y - (-1) = -\dfrac{3}{2}(x - 2) \;\Longrightarrow\; y + 1 = -\dfrac{3}{2}(x - 2)\]
Answer — slope-intercept form
\[y = -\dfrac{3}{2}x + 2\]
Distribute and subtract 1 from both sides.
Review
Take-aways
Slope is a ratio
\(m = \dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}\). Horizontal \(\Rightarrow m=0\); vertical \(\Rightarrow m\) undefined.
Three forms serve different uses
Slope-intercept for graphing, point-slope for building from data, standard for systems.
Parallel vs. perpendicular
Parallel: same slope. Perpendicular: opposite reciprocals (\(m_1 m_2 = -1\)).
Real-world modeling
Translate data to coordinates first, then pick point-slope form — the slope is the rate of change.
