1.4 Graphs of Functions
Objective: Read increase/decrease intervals, locate extrema, work with step and piecewise functions, and classify functions as even, odd, or neither.
increasing & decreasing extrema step functions piecewise even & odd
Concept
Increasing & Decreasing Functions
Increasing
An increase in \(x\) produces an _________increase in \(y\).
Decreasing
An increase in \(x\) produces a _________decrease in \(y\).
Formal definition
\(f\) is increasing on an interval iff for every \(x_1 < x_2\), \(f(x_1) < f(x_2)\). Decreasing flips the inequality on \(f\).
Example 1
Identify increasing, decreasing, or constant intervals
a) \(f(x) = x^3\)
Increasing on \((-\infty,\ \infty)\)
b) Piecewise graph
Increasing on \((-\infty,\ 0)\)
Decreasing on \([0,\ 2]\)
Constant on \((2,\ \infty)\)
Concept
Relative Extrema
Relative Maximum
A point where the function's \(y\)-value is ___________greater than all nearby points.
Relative Minimum
A point where the function's \(y\)-value is ___________less than all nearby points.
  • Relative (local) extrema: compared only to nearby points.
  • Absolute (global) extrema: highest / lowest points across the entire domain.
  • Every absolute extremum is also a relative one, but not vice versa.
Concept
Relative vs. Absolute Extrema
Hills & valleys are relative extrema — the highest/lowest points overall are the absolute extrema.
Notation tip
Report extrema as points \((x,\,y)\), or give the \(y\)-value "at \(x = \dots\)" so the audience knows both location and value.
Example 2
Temperature Model — Approximate Extrema
\(T(x) = 0.026x^3 - 1.03x^2 + 10.2x + 34\), \(0 \le x \le 24\), where \(x=0\) is 6 AM. Use a graphing utility to approximate the max and min temperatures.
Step 1 · Graph on [0, 24]
Plot \(T(x)\) and inspect the shape — cubic with a peak early, a dip overnight, then rising again near 24.
Step 2 · Approximate relative max
\[x \approx 6.6 \ \Rightarrow\ T \approx 64^\circ\text{F}\]
\(x=6.6\) corresponds to about 12:36 PM.
Step 3 · Approximate relative min
\[x \approx 19.8 \ \Rightarrow\ T \approx 34^\circ\text{F}\]
\(x=19.8\) corresponds to about 1:50 AM.
Step 4 · Check endpoints
\[T(0) = 34 \qquad T(24) \approx 45\]
Compare to relative extrema to decide which are absolute.
Conclusion
\[\text{Max } \approx 64^\circ\text{F at 12:36 PM},\ \ \text{Min } \approx 34^\circ\text{F at 6 AM / 1:50 AM}\]
Concept
Famous Step Functions
Floor · Greatest Integer
Output is the greatest integer \(\le x\).
Notation: \(\lfloor x \rfloor\) or \([x]\) or \([\![ x ]\!]\)
Also called: rounding down.
Ceiling · Least Integer
Output is the least integer \(\ge x\).
Notation: \(\lceil x \rceil\) or \(] x [\)
Also called: rounding up.

\(\lfloor -2.1 \rfloor = -3\) (go further left to \(-3\), not toward \(0\)). Integer inputs give \(\lfloor x \rfloor = \lceil x \rceil = x\).
Example 3
Graph the piecewise function
\[f(x) = \begin{cases} -x - 4, & x < 0 \\ x^2 - 4, & 0 \le x < 3 \\ 2x - 6, & x \ge 3 \end{cases}\]
Build the graph piece by piece
Linear piece left of \(0\), parabola from \(0\) up to \(x=3\), then another line going up.
Boundary checks
  • At \(x=0\): left piece gives \(-4\); right piece gives \(-4\). Closed dot at \((0,-4)\).
  • At \(x=3\): middle piece limits to \(5\) (open), right piece gives \(0\) (closed). Jump discontinuity.
  • Each boundary respects \(<\) (open) vs. \(\le\) (closed).
Concept
Even & Odd Functions
Even · \(y\)-axis symmetry
Definition: \(f(-x) = f(x)\)
Example: \(y = x^2,\ y = |x|,\ y = \cos x\)
Odd · origin symmetry
Definition: \(f(-x) = -f(x)\)
Example: \(y = x^3,\ y = x,\ y = \sin x\)
Algebraic test
Plug \(-x\) into \(f\) and simplify. If it equals \(f(x)\), even. If it equals \(-f(x)\), odd. Otherwise, neither.
Example 4
Is \(g(x) = x^3 - x\) even, odd, or neither?
Step 1 · Compute \(g(-x)\)
\[g(-x) = (-x)^3 - (-x) = -x^3 + x\]
Step 2 · Compare to \(g(x)\)
\[-x^3 + x \ne x^3 - x \ \Rightarrow\ \text{not even}\]
Step 3 · Compare to \(-g(x)\)
\[-g(x) = -(x^3 - x) = -x^3 + x \ \checkmark\]
Matches \(g(-x)\) exactly.
Conclusion
\[g(x) \text{ is \textbf{odd} — origin symmetry.}\]
Example 5
Is \(f(x) = |x|\) even, odd, or neither?
Step 1 · Compute \(f(-x)\)
\[f(-x) = |-x| = |x|\]
Step 2 · Compare to \(f(x)\)
\[|x| = f(x) \ \checkmark\]
Conclusion
\[f(x) \text{ is \textbf{even} — }y\text{-axis symmetry.}\]
Example 6
Is \(f(x) = x^3 - 1\) even, odd, or neither?
Step 1 · Compute \(f(-x)\)
\[f(-x) = (-x)^3 - 1 = -x^3 - 1\]
Step 2 · Compare to \(f(x)\)
\[-x^3 - 1 \ne x^3 - 1 \ \Rightarrow\ \text{not even}\]
Step 3 · Compare to \(-f(x)\)
\[-f(x) = -(x^3 - 1) = -x^3 + 1\]
\(-x^3 - 1 \ne -x^3 + 1\) — the constants disagree.
Conclusion
\[f(x) \text{ is \textbf{neither} even nor odd.}\]
Summary
Big Ideas · 1.4
Intervals from graphs
Scan left to right — "uphill" = increasing, "downhill" = decreasing, "flat" = constant.
Extrema
Relative = local hills/valleys. Absolute = highest/lowest over the whole domain. Always check endpoints.
Step functions
Floor \(\lfloor x \rfloor\) rounds down; ceiling \(\lceil x \rceil\) rounds up. Graphs are staircases with closed/open endpoints.
Piecewise graphing
Graph each piece over its restricted \(x\)-range. Respect \(<\) (open) and \(\le\) (closed) at boundaries.
Even test
\(f(-x) = f(x)\). Graphs are symmetric about the \(y\)-axis. Only even powers & \(|x|\).
Odd test
\(f(-x) = -f(x)\). Graphs are symmetric about the origin. Only odd powers, no constant term.
