8.3 Polar Form of Complex Numbers
Objective: Write complex numbers in polar form, multiply/divide using polar form, and apply De Moivre's Theorem.
modulus argument cis notation De Moivre's Theorem
Reference
Modulus of a Complex Number
Recall from Unit 2.5: every complex number \(z = a + bi\) is a point \((a,b)\) on the complex plane. The horizontal axis is the real axis and the vertical axis is the imaginary axis.
Modulus
\[|z| = |a + bi| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}\]
  • Geometrically, \(|z|\) is the __________distance from the origin to \(z\) in the complex plane.
Examples 1 & 2
Finding the Modulus
Find (a) \(|\sqrt{5} - i|\) and (b) \(|3 - 4i|\). OpenStax
Part (a)
\[|\sqrt{5} - i| = \sqrt{(\sqrt{5})^2 + (-1)^2} = \sqrt{5 + 1} = \boxed{\sqrt{6}}\]
Part (b)
\[|3 - 4i| = \sqrt{3^2 + (-4)^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = \sqrt{25} = \boxed{5}\]
Reference
Polar Form of a Complex Number
Polar Form
\[z = r(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)\]
cis Shorthand
\[r(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta) = r\,\text{cis}\,\theta\]
  • \(r = |z| =\) ______________\(\sqrt{a^2 + b^2}\) is the modulus.
  • \(\theta\) is the argument: \(\tan\theta =\) ________\(b/a\) (adjust for quadrant).
  • Converting back: \(a =\) ____________\(r\cos\theta\) and \(b = r\sin\theta\).
Examples 3 & 4
Convert to Polar Form
Write in polar form: (a) \(4i\) and (b) \(-4 + 4i\). OpenStax
Part (a): \(z = 0 + 4i\)
\[r = \sqrt{0 + 16} = 4, \quad \theta = \frac{\pi}{2} \;\text{(positive imaginary axis)}\]
\(a = 0\), so point is on the y-axis.
\(\boxed{z = 4\,\text{cis}\!\left(\tfrac{\pi}{2}\right)}\)
Part (b): \(z = -4 + 4i\)
\[r = \sqrt{16 + 16} = 4\sqrt{2}, \quad \tan\theta = \frac{4}{-4} = -1 \;\Longrightarrow\; \theta = \frac{3\pi}{4} \;\text{(QII)}\]
Reference angle is \(\pi/4\); point is in QII so \(\theta = \pi - \pi/4\).
\(\boxed{z = 4\sqrt{2}\,\text{cis}\!\left(\tfrac{3\pi}{4}\right)}\)
Example 5
Convert to Rectangular Form
Convert \(z = 12\!\left(\cos\frac{\pi}{6} + i\sin\frac{\pi}{6}\right)\) to rectangular form. OpenStax
Evaluate trig values
\[a = 12\cos\frac{\pi}{6} = 12 \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = 6\sqrt{3}\]
\[b = 12\sin\frac{\pi}{6} = 12 \cdot \frac{1}{2} = 6\]
\(\boxed{z = 6\sqrt{3} + 6i}\)
Reference
Multiplication & Division in Polar Form
Let \(z_1 = r_1\,\text{cis}\,\theta_1\) and \(z_2 = r_2\,\text{cis}\,\theta_2\).
Product
\[z_1 z_2 = r_1 r_2\,\text{cis}(\theta_1 + \theta_2)\]
Quotient
\[\frac{z_1}{z_2} = \frac{r_1}{r_2}\,\text{cis}(\theta_1 - \theta_2)\]
  • Product: Multiply moduli, ________add arguments.
  • Quotient: Divide moduli, ____________subtract arguments.
Example 6
Multiplying Complex Numbers in Polar Form
Find \(z_1 z_2\) where \(z_1 = 4\,\text{cis}(80^\circ)\) and \(z_2 = 2\,\text{cis}(145^\circ)\). OpenStax
Apply product rule
\[z_1 z_2 = (4)(2)\,\text{cis}(80^\circ + 145^\circ) = 8\,\text{cis}(225^\circ)\]
Convert to rectangular
\[\cos 225^\circ = -\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}, \quad \sin 225^\circ = -\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\]
\[\boxed{z_1 z_2 = -4\sqrt{2} - 4\sqrt{2}\,i}\]
Example 7
Dividing Complex Numbers in Polar Form
Find \(\dfrac{z_1}{z_2}\) where \(z_1 = 2\,\text{cis}(213^\circ)\) and \(z_2 = 4\,\text{cis}(33^\circ)\). OpenStax
Apply quotient rule
\[\frac{z_1}{z_2} = \frac{2}{4}\,\text{cis}(213^\circ - 33^\circ) = \frac{1}{2}\,\text{cis}(180^\circ)\]
\[\frac{1}{2}(\cos 180^\circ + i\sin 180^\circ) = \frac{1}{2}(-1 + 0i) = \boxed{-\frac{1}{2}}\]
A purely real result — the argument \(180^\circ\) puts us on the negative real axis.
Honors
De Moivre's Theorem & nth Roots
Powers
\[z^n = r^n\,\text{cis}(n\theta)\]
nth Roots
\[z^{1/n} = r^{1/n}\,\text{cis}\!\left(\frac{\theta + 2\pi k}{n}\right), \quad k = 0, 1, \ldots, n-1\]
Example: Evaluate \((1+i)^5\)
\[r = \sqrt{2}, \; \theta = \frac{\pi}{4} \;\Longrightarrow\; (\sqrt{2})^5\,\text{cis}\!\left(\frac{5\pi}{4}\right) = 4\sqrt{2}\,\text{cis}\!\left(\frac{5\pi}{4}\right)\]
Result
\[4\sqrt{2}\!\left(-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} - \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\,i\right) = \boxed{-4 - 4i}\]
Example: Cube roots of \(8\,\text{cis}\!\left(\frac{2\pi}{3}\right)\)
\[z^{1/3} = 2\,\text{cis}\!\left(\frac{2\pi/3 + 2\pi k}{3}\right) \quad k=0{:}\; 2\,\text{cis}(40^\circ), \; k=1{:}\; 2\,\text{cis}(160^\circ), \; k=2{:}\; 2\,\text{cis}(280^\circ)\]
Three roots equally spaced \(120^\circ\) apart on a circle of radius 2.
