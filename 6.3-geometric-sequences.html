6.3 Geometric Sequences
Objective: Write geometric nth-term formulas, use geometric means, and evaluate finite and infinite geometric series.
common rationth termgeometric meanfinite sumsinfinite sums
Reference
Geometric Sequence Formulas
ratio
\[r = \dfrac{a_n}{a_{n-1}}\]
nth term
\[a_n = a_1r^{\,n-1}\]
finite sum
\[S_n = \dfrac{a_1(1-r^n)}{1-r}, \quad r \ne 1\]
infinite sum
\[S_{\infty} = \dfrac{a_1}{1-r}, \quad |r| < 1\]
The geometric mean between two positive numbers \(a\) and \(b\) is \(\sqrt{ab}\).
Example 1
Recognizing a Geometric Rule
Write an explicit formula for the sequence \(3, 6, 12, 24, \dots\).
Find the Common Ratio
\[\dfrac{6}{3} = \dfrac{12}{6} = \dfrac{24}{12} = 2\]
Use the nth-Term Formula
\[a_n = a_1r^{n-1} = 3(2)^{n-1}\]
Answer
\[a_n = 3\cdot 2^{n-1}\]
Example 2
Growth by a Percent Ratio
Find the 15th term of the geometric sequence with \(a_1 = 20\) and \(r = 1.05\).
Use the nth-Term Formula
\[a_{15} = 20(1.05)^{14}\]
Evaluate
\[a_{15} \approx 39.60\]
Answer
\[a_{15} \approx 39.60\]
Example 3
Geometric Means
Find three geometric means between \(3.12\) and \(49.92\).
Set Up the Five-Term Sequence
\[3.12,\ \square,\ \square,\ \square,\ 49.92\]
Five terms create four equal ratio jumps.
Solve for the Ratio
\[3.12r^4 = 49.92 \quad \Rightarrow \quad r^4 = 16 \quad \Rightarrow \quad r = 2\]
Fill in the Means
\[3.12,\ 6.24,\ 12.48,\ 24.96,\ 49.92\]
Concept
Finite and Infinite Geometric Sums
Multiplying a geometric sum by the common ratio makes almost every term line up, which is why the finite-sum formula works so cleanly.
Finite Sum
\[S_n = a_1 + a_1r + a_1r^2 + \dots + a_1r^{n-1}\]
Subtract the Shifted Copy
\[(1-r)S_n = a_1 - a_1r^n\]
Infinite Sum
When \(|r|<1\), the term \(a_1r^n\) approaches \(0\), so the sum approaches a finite value.
Example 4
Summing a Finite Geometric Series
Find the exact sum of the first 12 terms of \(8, 4, 2, \dots\).
Identify the Data
  • \(a_1 = 8\)
  • \(r = \dfrac{1}{2}\)
  • \(n = 12\)
Use the Finite-Sum Formula
\[S_{12} = \dfrac{8\left(1-\left(\frac12\right)^{12}\right)}{1-\frac12}\]
Simplify Exactly
\[S_{12} = 16\left(1 - \dfrac{1}{4096}\right) = \dfrac{4095}{256}\]
Example 5
Solving for the Number of Terms
A geometric series has first term \(1\), common ratio \(3\), and finite sum \(29524\). Find \(n\).
Write the Sum Formula
\[29524 = \dfrac{1(1-3^n)}{1-3} = \dfrac{3^n - 1}{2}\]
Solve for \(3^n\)
\[59048 = 3^n - 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad 59049 = 3^n\]
Recognize the Power
\[59049 = 3^{10}\]
Answer
\[n = 10\]
Example 6
Infinite Geometric Series
Find the sum of the infinite geometric series \(6 + 3 + 1.5 + 0.75 + \dots\).
Check the Ratio
\[r = \dfrac{3}{6} = \dfrac12\]
\(|r| < 1\), so the infinite sum exists.
Use the Infinite-Sum Formula
\[S_{\infty} = \dfrac{a_1}{1-r} = \dfrac{6}{1-\frac12}\]
Answer
\[S_{\infty} = 12\]
