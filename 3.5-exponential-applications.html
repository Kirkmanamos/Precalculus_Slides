Unit 3 · Exponential & Logarithmic Functions
3.5 Exponential Applications
Objective: Apply exponential models — compound interest, continuous growth, exponential decay, and half-life — to solve real-world problems by setting up the equation and solving with logarithms.
doubling time continuous growth population models bacteria growth half-life
Reference
Common Exponential Models
Compound Interest
\[A = P\!\left(1 + \dfrac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}\]
Continuous Compounding
\[A = P\,e^{\,rt}\]
Growth / Decay
\[y = a\,e^{\,kt}\]
Variables
\(A\) = final amount  ·  \(P\) = principal  ·  \(r\) = annual rate (decimal)  ·  \(n\) = compoundings/year  ·  \(t\) = years
\(a\) = initial amount  ·  \(k\) = proportionality constant (positive ⇒ growth, negative ⇒ decay)
Solution recipe
Substitute the known values, isolate the exponential, take \(\ln\) of both sides, then solve for the unknown.
Example 1
How long for $500 at 6.75% continuous to double?
You deposit $500 in an account paying \(6.75\%\) compounded continuously. How long until the balance doubles?
Set up the equation
\[A = Pe^{rt} \;\Longrightarrow\; 1000 = 500\,e^{0.0675t}\]
Isolate the exponential
\[e^{0.0675t} = 2\]
Take \(\ln\) of both sides
\[0.0675t = \ln 2\]
Solve for \(t\)
\[t = \dfrac{\ln 2}{0.0675}\]
Approximate
\[t \approx \dfrac{0.6931}{0.0675} \approx 10.27 \text{ years}\]
Example 2
In what year will the tuition double?
A private college's tuition is modeled by \(M(t) = 40{,}000(1.072)^{t}\), where \(t\) is the number of years since 2020. When will tuition be double its 2020 amount?
Set up the equation
\[80{,}000 = 40{,}000(1.072)^{t} \;\Longrightarrow\; (1.072)^{t} = 2\]
Take \(\ln\) of both sides
\[t \ln(1.072) = \ln 2\]
Solve for \(t\)
\[t = \dfrac{\ln 2}{\ln 1.072} \approx \dfrac{0.6931}{0.0695} \approx 9.97 \text{ years}\]
Answer
\[2020 + 10 = \boxed{2030}\]
Example 3
When will the world population reach 10 billion?
\(P = 7021.7\,e^{\,0.01076 t}\), where \(P\) is the population in millions and \(t = 10\) represents the year 2010. Find when \(P = 10{,}000\) million.
Set up the equation
\[10{,}000 = 7021.7\,e^{\,0.01076 t}\]
Isolate the exponential
\[e^{\,0.01076 t} = \dfrac{10{,}000}{7021.7} \approx 1.4242\]
Take \(\ln\) of both sides
\[0.01076\,t = \ln(1.4242) \approx 0.35369\]
Solve for \(t\)
\[t \approx \dfrac{0.35369}{0.01076} \approx 32.87\]
Interpret
Since \(t = 10\) is 2010, the variable \(t\) is years since 2000. \(t \approx 32.87 \Rightarrow\) year \(\approx 2033\).
Example 4
Bacteria — initial count 500, growth rate 40% per hour
(a) Find a function modeling the count after \(t\) hours. (b) Find the count after 10 hours. (c) When does the count reach 80,000?
(a) Identify \(a\) and \(k\) in \(y = a\,e^{kt}\)
Initial \(a = 500\); continuous rate \(k = 0.40\).
(a) Model
\[N(t) = 500\,e^{\,0.40\,t}\]
(b) Evaluate at \(t = 10\)
\[N(10) = 500\,e^{\,4} \approx 500(54.598)\]
(b) Answer
\[N(10) \approx 27{,}299\ \text{bacteria}\]
(c) Solve \(500\,e^{\,0.40 t} = 80{,}000\)
\[e^{\,0.40 t} = 160 \;\Longrightarrow\; 0.40\,t = \ln 160 \approx 5.0752\]
(c) Answer
\[t \approx \dfrac{5.0752}{0.40} \approx 12.69 \text{ hours}\]
Example 5
Polonium-210 — half-life 140 days, sample 300 mg
(a) Find \(y = a\,e^{kt}\) for the mass remaining after \(t\) days. (b) How long until the sample decays to 200 mg?
(a) Use \(a = 300\) and the half-life condition
\[\tfrac{1}{2}(300) = 300\,e^{\,140k} \;\Longrightarrow\; e^{\,140k} = \tfrac{1}{2}\]
(a) Solve for \(k\)
\[140k = \ln \tfrac{1}{2} \;\Longrightarrow\; k = -\dfrac{\ln 2}{140} \approx -0.00495\]
(a) Model
\[y = 300\,e^{\,-\frac{\ln 2}{140}\,t} \;\approx\; 300\,e^{\,-0.00495\,t}\]
(b) Set \(y = 200\)
\[200 = 300\,e^{\,kt} \;\Longrightarrow\; e^{\,kt} = \tfrac{2}{3}\]
(b) Take \(\ln\) and solve
\[t = \dfrac{\ln \tfrac{2}{3}}{k} = \dfrac{140\,\ln \tfrac{3}{2}}{\ln 2}\]
(b) Approximate
\[t \approx \dfrac{140(0.4055)}{0.6931} \approx 81.9 \text{ days}\]
