8.6 Vectors
Objective: Write vectors in component form, find magnitude and direction, perform operations, and compute dot products.
component form magnitude & direction unit vectors dot product
Reference
Vectors and Scalars
  • A scalar is a quantity described by ______________magnitude only.
  • A vector is described by both magnitude and ______________direction.
Component Form
\[\mathbf{v} = \langle a, b \rangle\]
From Two Points
\[\mathbf{v} = \langle x_2 - x_1,\; y_2 - y_1 \rangle\]
Example 1
Component Form from Two Points
Find the component form of the vector with initial point \(P(2, 3)\) and terminal point \(Q(6, 4)\). OpenStax
Subtract coordinates
\[\mathbf{v} = \langle 6 - 2,\; 4 - 3 \rangle = \boxed{\langle 4, 1 \rangle}\]
Reference
Magnitude & Direction
Magnitude
\[|\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}\]
Direction
\[\tan\theta = \frac{b}{a} \quad \text{(check quadrant)}\]
From Mag & Angle
\[a = |\mathbf{v}|\cos\theta, \quad b = |\mathbf{v}|\sin\theta\]
Examples 2 & 3
Magnitude/Direction & Components from Angle
(a) Find the magnitude and direction of \(\mathbf{v} = \langle 3, 4 \rangle\). (b) Write the vector with magnitude 7 and direction \(30^\circ\) in component form. OpenStax
Part (a)
\[|\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{9 + 16} = \boxed{5}, \qquad \theta = \arctan\!\left(\frac{4}{3}\right) \approx \boxed{53.1^\circ}\]
Part (b)
\[a = 7\cos 30^\circ = \frac{7\sqrt{3}}{2}, \quad b = 7\sin 30^\circ = \frac{7}{2}\]
\(\mathbf{v} = \left\langle \dfrac{7\sqrt{3}}{2},\; \dfrac{7}{2} \right\rangle\)
Reference
Vector Operations
Let \(\mathbf{u} = \langle a, b \rangle\), \(\mathbf{v} = \langle c, d \rangle\), and \(k\) a scalar.
Operation Formula Geometric Meaning
Addition\(\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v} = \langle a+c,\; b+d \rangle\)Tip-to-tail
Subtraction\(\mathbf{u} - \mathbf{v} = \langle a-c,\; b-d \rangle\)Reverse \(\mathbf{v}\), then add
Scalar Mult.\(k\mathbf{v} = \langle kc,\; kd \rangle\)Scales magnitude; reverses if \(k < 0\)
Example 4
Vector Arithmetic
Let \(\mathbf{u} = \langle 3, -2 \rangle\) and \(\mathbf{v} = \langle -1, 4 \rangle\). Find (a) \(\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v}\), (b) \(\mathbf{u} - \mathbf{v}\), (c) \(3\mathbf{v}\). OpenStax
(a) Addition
\[\langle 3+(-1),\; -2+4 \rangle = \boxed{\langle 2, 2 \rangle}\]
(b) Subtraction
\[\langle 3-(-1),\; -2-4 \rangle = \boxed{\langle 4, -6 \rangle}\]
(c) Scalar Multiplication
\[3\langle -1, 4 \rangle = \boxed{\langle -3, 12 \rangle}\]
Reference
Standard Unit Vectors & \(\mathbf{i}, \mathbf{j}\) Notation
Unit Vectors
\[\mathbf{i} = \langle 1, 0 \rangle, \qquad \mathbf{j} = \langle 0, 1 \rangle\]
Any Vector
\[\mathbf{v} = \langle a, b \rangle = a\,\mathbf{i} + b\,\mathbf{j}\]
Unit Vector in Direction of \(\mathbf{v}\)
\[\hat{\mathbf{v}} = \frac{\mathbf{v}}{|\mathbf{v}|} = \left\langle \frac{a}{|\mathbf{v}|},\; \frac{b}{|\mathbf{v}|} \right\rangle\]
  • The unit vector always has magnitude ____\(1\).
Example 5
Finding a Unit Vector
Find the unit vector in the direction of \(\mathbf{v} = \langle -5, 12 \rangle\). Write in component form and \(\mathbf{i}, \mathbf{j}\) notation. OpenStax
Divide by magnitude
\[|\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{25 + 144} = \sqrt{169} = 13\]
\[\hat{\mathbf{v}} = \left\langle \frac{-5}{13},\; \frac{12}{13} \right\rangle = \boxed{-\frac{5}{13}\,\mathbf{i} + \frac{12}{13}\,\mathbf{j}}\]
Verify: \(\left(\frac{-5}{13}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{12}{13}\right)^2 = \frac{25+144}{169} = 1\;\checkmark\)
Honors
Dot Product & Angle Between Vectors
Dot Product
\[\mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{u} = ac + bd\]
Angle
\[\cos\theta = \frac{\mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{u}}{|\mathbf{v}|\,|\mathbf{u}|}\]
Example: \(\mathbf{v} = \langle 5, 12 \rangle\) and \(\mathbf{u} = \langle -3, 4 \rangle\)
\[\mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{u} = (5)(-3) + (12)(4) = -15 + 48 = \boxed{33}\]
Find the angle
\[|\mathbf{v}| = 13, \quad |\mathbf{u}| = 5 \;\Longrightarrow\; \cos\theta = \frac{33}{65} \;\Longrightarrow\; \theta \approx \boxed{59.5^\circ}\]
Perpendicular Vectors
\(\mathbf{v} \perp \mathbf{u}\) if and only if \(\mathbf{v} \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0\).
Example 6
Airplane Ground Speed & Bearing
An airplane flies at 200 mph on compass bearing \(140^\circ\). A 16.2 mph wind blows due north. Find the actual ground speed and bearing. OpenStax
Convert to standard angle
\[\text{Standard angle} = 90^\circ - 140^\circ = -50^\circ\]
\[\text{Airplane: } \langle 200\cos(-50^\circ),\; 200\sin(-50^\circ) \rangle \approx \langle 128.56,\; -153.21 \rangle\]
Add wind vector
\[\text{Wind: } \langle 0, 16.2 \rangle \;\Longrightarrow\; \text{Sum: } \langle 128.56,\; -137.01 \rangle\]
Ground speed & bearing
\[\text{Speed} = \sqrt{128.56^2 + 137.01^2} \approx \boxed{187.9 \;\text{mph}}\]
\[\text{Bearing} = 90^\circ - \arctan\!\left(\frac{-137.01}{128.56}\right) \approx \boxed{136.8^\circ}\]
