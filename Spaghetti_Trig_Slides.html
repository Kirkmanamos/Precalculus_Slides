Spaghetti Sine
Graph

Building \(y = \sin(\theta)\) from the Unit Circle

Required Materials

📄

2 sheets, taped
🍝

Uncooked, stiff
🧵

~12 inches
🖊️
Marker
Any color
📏
Ruler
For straight lines
🥣
Bowl
For tracing circle

1. Prepare Your Unit Circle

  • Trace a bowl and draw your axes through the center.
  • Label the origin \((0,0)\) and rightmost point \((1,0)\).
  • Estimate angles using clock positions:
    • \(0 \approx 3\) o'clock
    • \(\frac{\pi}{6} \approx 2\) o'clock
    • \(\frac{\pi}{3} \approx 1\) o'clock
    • \(\frac{\pi}{2} \approx 12\) o'clock

Unit Circle

2. The String & Graph Paper

  • The String: Wrap string around the circle. Mark it at each angle (\(0\) to \(2\pi\)). This becomes your x-axis.
  • The Paper: Tape sheets long-ways. Draw a horizontal axis labeled \(\theta\) and a vertical axis for \(y = \sin(\theta)\).
  • Transfer string marks to the horizontal axis to get evenly-spaced angle positions.
↓ unrolls to ↓
\(0\) \(\frac{\pi}{2}\) \(\pi\) \(\frac{3\pi}{2}\) \(2\pi\)

3. Measuring with Spaghetti

  • Hold spaghetti vertically from the x-axis to the point on the circle.
  • Break it at that exact height — this is the \(\sin(\theta)\) value.
  • Transfer the piece to your graph paper at the matching \(\theta\) mark.
  • Repeat for every labeled angle from \(0\) to \(2\pi\).

4. Building the Wave

  • Positive sine values: pasta goes UP from the axis.
  • Negative sine values: pasta goes DOWN below the axis.
  • Connect the tips of the pasta with a smooth curve.
  • You've turned circle heights into a sine wave!

Final Reflection

⚪ = ∿
  • Where is \(\sin(\theta)\) at its maximum?
  • Where does \(\sin(\theta)\) equal zero?
  • Where is it at its minimum?
  • Key Idea: Moving around a circle creates repeating vertical motion — a wave.
