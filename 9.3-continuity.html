9.3 Continuity
Objective: State the three conditions of continuity, identify removable and jump discontinuities, and determine continuity.
three conditions removable discontinuity jump discontinuity
Reference
Continuity at a Point
A function \(f\) is continuous at \(x = a\) if all three conditions hold:
Condition 1
__________________________\(f(a)\) exists (is defined)
Condition 2
__________________________\(\lim_{x \to a} f(x)\) exists
Condition 3
__________________________\(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a)\)
Key Idea
If any condition fails, \(f\) is discontinuous at \(x = a\). A function continuous everywhere has no holes or breaks.
Reference
Types of Discontinuity
Jump Discontinuity
Both one-sided limits exist but are not equal: \(\lim_{x \to a^-} f(x) \ne \lim_{x \to a^+} f(x)\)

The graph has a ________jump at \(x = a\). Cannot be fixed.
Removable Discontinuity
The two-sided limit exists, but \(f(a)\) is undefined or \(f(a) \ne \lim f\).

The graph has a ________hole at \(x = a\). Can be fixed by redefining \(f(a)\).
Example 1
Testing for Continuity (Removable)
Is \(f(x) = \dfrac{x^2 - 25}{x - 5}\) continuous at \(x = 5\)? Classify any discontinuity. OpenStax
Check conditions
Condition 1: \(f(5) = \frac{0}{0}\) — undefined. ❌ Fails.
Condition 2: \(\frac{(x-5)(x+5)}{x-5} = x + 5\); \(\lim_{x \to 5}(x+5) = 10\). ✓
Condition 3: Cannot check — \(f(5)\) is undefined.
Conclusion
Removable discontinuity at \(x = 5\). Redefine \(f(5) = 10\) to remove it.
Example 2
Testing for Continuity (Jump)
Is \(f(x) = \begin{cases} 4x & x \le 3 \\ 8 + x & x > 3 \end{cases}\) continuous at \(x = 3\)? OpenStax
Check conditions
Condition 1: \(f(3) = 4(3) = 12\). ✓
Condition 2: \(\lim_{x \to 3^-} 4x = 12\) and \(\lim_{x \to 3^+}(8+x) = 11\). Left \(\ne\) Right. ❌
Conclusion
Jump discontinuity at \(x = 3\). Cannot be removed.
Example 3
A Continuous Piecewise Function
Is \(f(x) = \begin{cases} x + 1 & x < 2 \\ 3 & x = 2 \\ x^2 - 1 & x > 2 \end{cases}\) continuous at \(x = 2\)? OpenStax
All three conditions
Condition 1: \(f(2) = 3\). ✓
Condition 2: \(\lim_{x\to2^-}(x+1) = 3\) and \(\lim_{x\to2^+}(x^2-1) = 3\). Equal — limit = 3. ✓
Condition 3: \(\lim_{x\to2} f(x) = 3 = f(2)\). ✓

\(f\) is continuous at \(x = 2\). ✓
Example 4
Removable Discontinuity (Undefined)
Is \(f(x) = \begin{cases} \sin x & x < 0 \\ x^3 & x > 0 \end{cases}\) continuous at \(x = 0\)? OpenStax
Check conditions
Condition 1: \(f(0)\) is undefined — neither piece covers \(x = 0\). ❌
Condition 2: \(\lim_{x\to0^-}\sin x = 0\) and \(\lim_{x\to0^+} x^3 = 0\). Limit = 0. ✓

Conclusion: Removable discontinuity at \(x = 0\). Define \(f(0) = 0\) to remove it.
Concept

Families of Continuous Functions

Continuous Everywhere
  • Polynomials
  • Exponentials (\(e^x, 2^x\))
  • \(\sin x\) and \(\cos x\)
Continuous on Their Domain
  • Logarithms (domain \(x > 0\))
  • Tangent (undef at \(\frac{\pi}{2} + k\pi\))
  • Rationals (undef where denom = 0)

Can You Now...?
Review these core concepts.
Three Conditions
\(f(a)\) exists, \(\lim f\) exists, and they are equal. All three must hold.
Classify Discontinuities
Removable = hole (fixable). Jump = left \(\ne\) right (not fixable).
Common Continuous Families
Polynomials, exponentials, sine/cosine are always continuous. Rationals, logs, tangent are continuous on their domain.
