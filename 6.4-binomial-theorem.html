Combination Formula
\[\binom{n}{r} = \dfrac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}\]
Calculator
On many calculators, use nCr to find \(\binom{n}{r}\).
Row 4 Example
Row \(4\) gives \(1, 4, 6, 4, 1\), which are the coefficients of \((a+b)^4\).
Example 1
Expanding with Pascal's Triangle
Use Pascal's Triangle to expand \((x + 2)^4\).
Choose the Row of Coefficients
\[1,\ 4,\ 6,\ 4,\ 1\]
Row \(4\) matches the exponent \(4\).
Match the Power Pattern
\[x^4\cdot 2^0,\ x^3\cdot 2^1,\ x^2\cdot 2^2,\ x\cdot 2^3,\ 2^4\]
Multiply the Coefficients In
\[(x+2)^4 = x^4 + 8x^3 + 24x^2 + 32x + 16\]
Reference
The Binomial Theorem
theorem
\[(a+b)^n = \sum_{r=0}^{n}\binom{n}{r}a^{\,n-r}b^r\]
term
\[T_{r+1} = \binom{n}{r}a^{\,n-r}b^r\]
The term number is always one more than \(r\), so the 6th term uses \(r=5\).
How to Find a Specific Term
  1. Set \(r = \text{term number} - 1\)
  2. Plug \(a\), \(b\), \(n\), \(r\) into \(T_{r+1} = \binom{n}{r}a^{n-r}b^r\)
  3. Simplify powers and coefficients separately
Example 2
Expanding with the Binomial Theorem
Use the Binomial Theorem to expand \((x - 2y)^5\).
Identify \(a\), \(b\), and \(n\)
\[a = x,\qquad b = -2y,\qquad n = 5\]
Apply the Theorem
\[(x-2y)^5 = \sum_{r=0}^{5}\binom{5}{r}x^{5-r}(-2y)^r\]
Simplify Each Term
\[(x-2y)^5 = x^5 - 10x^4y + 40x^3y^2 - 80x^2y^3 + 80xy^4 - 32y^5\]
Watch Out
When \(b\) is negative, the signs alternate: \((-b)^0 = +\), \((-b)^1 = -\), \((-b)^2 = +\), \((-b)^3 = -\), …
Example 3
Another Full Expansion
Write the expansion of \((3x+1)^5\).
Identify \(a\), \(b\), and \(n\)
\[a = 3x,\qquad b = 1,\qquad n = 5\]
Apply the Theorem
\[(3x)^5 + 5(3x)^4(1) + 10(3x)^3(1)^2 + 10(3x)^2(1)^3 + 5(3x)(1)^4 + 1\]
Simplify
\[(3x+1)^5 = 243x^5 + 405x^4 + 270x^3 + 90x^2 + 15x + 1\]
Example 4
Finding a Specific Term
Find the 6th term of \((x + 2y)^8\).
Match the Term Number to \(r\)
\[\text{6th term} \Rightarrow r = 5\]
Use the General Term
\[T_6 = \binom{8}{5}x^{8-5}(2y)^5\]
Simplify
\[T_6 = 56\cdot x^3\cdot 32y^5 = 1792\,x^3 y^5\]
Example 5
Finding Another Specific Term
Find the 4th term of \((3x-5)^9\).
Match the Term Number to \(r\)
\[\text{4th term} \Rightarrow r = 3\]
Use the General Term
\[T_4 = \binom{9}{3}(3x)^{6}(-5)^3 = 84 \cdot 729x^6 \cdot (-125)\]
Answer
\[T_4 = -7{,}654{,}500\,x^6\]
Example 6
Two-Variable Coefficient
Find the coefficient of \(x^7 y^{12}\) in the expansion of \((2x-3y^3)^{11}\).
Set Up the General Term
\[T_{r+1} = \binom{11}{r}(2x)^{11-r}(-3y^3)^r\]
Match the Exponents
  • \(x\)-exponent: \(11-r = 7 \implies r = 4\)
  • \(y\)-exponent: \(3r = 12\) ✓
Substitute \(r = 4\)
\[\binom{11}{4}(2x)^7(-3y^3)^4 = 330 \cdot 128x^7 \cdot 81y^{12}\]
Answer
\[\text{Coefficient} = 3{,}421{,}440\]

Your Turn
Practice: Finding a Coefficient
Find the coefficient of \(x^2\) in the expansion of \((2x + 3)^5\).
Identify Which Power of \(3\) You Need
\[x^2 \text{ means } 5-r = 2 \Rightarrow r = 3\]
Use the Matching Term
\[\binom{5}{3}(2x)^2(3)^3 = 10 \cdot 4x^2 \cdot 27\]
Answer
\[\text{Coefficient of }x^2 = 1080\]
Review
Can You Now...?
Use this checklist to make sure you are choosing the right binomial-theorem move before you expand or isolate a term.
Spot the Pattern
Recognize that \((a+b)^n\) has \(n+1\) terms, symmetric coefficients, and exponents that move in opposite directions.
Use Pascal or Theorem
Choose Pascal's Triangle for small full expansions and the Binomial Theorem when you need a general or specific-term formula.
Track the Exponents
Check that the exponent on the first factor decreases while the exponent on the second factor increases.
Match Term Number to \(r\)
For the \(k\)th term, use \(r = k-1\) in \(T_{r+1} = \binom{n}{r}a^{n-r}b^r\).
Separate Coefficients and Variables
Simplify the numerical coefficient and the variable powers in separate steps to avoid sign and exponent mistakes.
Target the Right Power
For coefficient questions, match the required exponent first, then compute only that one term.
Most errors in these problems come from using the wrong \(r\) or missing a negative sign.
