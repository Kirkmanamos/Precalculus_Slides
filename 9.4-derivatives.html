9.4 Derivatives
Objective: Compute average & instantaneous rates of change, find derivatives from the definition, and write tangent-line equations.
difference quotient limit definition tangent line velocity
Reference
Average Rate of Change
Difference Quotient
\[\text{AROC} = \frac{f(a+h) - f(a)}{h}\]
  • Geometrically: slope of the ____________secant line connecting \((a, f(a))\) and \((a+h, f(a+h))\).
  • Also written as \(\dfrac{f(b) - f(a)}{b - a}\) where \(b = a + h\).
Example 1
Average Rate of Change
Find the average rate of change of \(f\) between the points \((2, -6)\) and \((-1, 5)\). OpenStax
Apply the formula
\[\text{AROC} = \frac{5 - (-6)}{-1 - 2} = \frac{11}{-3} = \boxed{-\frac{11}{3}}\]
Reference
The Derivative at a Point
Definition
\[f'(a) = \lim_{h \to 0}\frac{f(a+h) - f(a)}{h}\]
  • \(f'(a)\) is the instantaneous rate of change of \(f\) at \(x = a\).
  • Geometrically: slope of the ______________tangent line at \((a, f(a))\).
  • If the limit DNE at \(x = a\), \(f\) is ______________________not differentiable there.
Procedure
1. Write \(f(a+h)\) by substituting \(a+h\) for \(x\).   2. Subtract \(f(a)\) and simplify.   3. Divide by \(h\) and cancel.   4. Take the limit as \(h \to 0\).
Example 2
Derivative from the Definition
Find \(f'(a)\) for \(f(x) = x^2 - 3x + 5\). OpenStax
Step 1: Expand \(f(a+h)\)
\[f(a+h) = (a+h)^2 - 3(a+h) + 5 = a^2 + 2ah + h^2 - 3a - 3h + 5\]
Step 2: Subtract and simplify
\[f(a+h) - f(a) = 2ah + h^2 - 3h = h(2a + h - 3)\]
Steps 3–4: Divide and take limit
\[\frac{h(2a + h - 3)}{h} = 2a + h - 3 \;\xrightarrow{h \to 0}\; \boxed{f'(a) = 2a - 3}\]
Example 3
Derivative of a Rational Function
Find \(f'(a)\) for \(f(x) = \dfrac{3 + x}{2 - x}\). OpenStax
Set up and simplify
\[\frac{f(a+h)-f(a)}{h} = \frac{1}{h}\left[\frac{3+a+h}{2-a-h} - \frac{3+a}{2-a}\right] = \frac{(3+a+h)(2-a)-(3+a)(2-a-h)}{h(2-a-h)(2-a)}\]
\[\text{Numerator} = 5h \;\Longrightarrow\; \frac{5h}{h(2-a-h)(2-a)} = \frac{5}{(2-a-h)(2-a)}\]
Take the limit
\[\lim_{h \to 0}\frac{5}{(2-a-h)(2-a)} = \boxed{f'(a) = \frac{5}{(2-a)^2}}\]
Example 4
Derivative at a Specific Point (Conjugate)
Find \(f'(36)\) for \(f(x) = 4\sqrt{x}\). OpenStax
Set up and use conjugate
\[\frac{4\sqrt{36+h} - 24}{h}\cdot\frac{4\sqrt{36+h}+24}{4\sqrt{36+h}+24} = \frac{16(36+h)-576}{h(4\sqrt{36+h}+24)} = \frac{16h}{h(4\sqrt{36+h}+24)}\]
Take the limit
\[\frac{16}{4\sqrt{36}+24} = \frac{16}{24+24} = \frac{16}{48} = \boxed{\frac{1}{3}}\]

Tangent Line Equation
Interactive Explorer ↗
Tangent Line
\[y = f'(a)(x - a) + f(a)\]
  • Point-slope form with slope ________\(f'(a)\) through the point ____________\((a, f(a))\).
Example 5
Equation of the Tangent Line
Find the equation of the tangent line to \(f(x) = x^2 - 4x\) at \(x = 3\). OpenStax
Find slope and point
\[f'(a) = 2a - 4 \;\Longrightarrow\; f'(3) = 2 \qquad f(3) = 9 - 12 = -3\]
Slope = 2, point = \((3, -3)\)
Write equation
\[y = 2(x - 3) + (-3) \;\Longrightarrow\; \boxed{y = 2x - 9}\]
Reference
Instantaneous Velocity
Velocity
\[s'(a) = \lim_{h \to 0}\frac{s(a+h) - s(a)}{h}\]
  • Positive \(s'(a)\): moving in the __________positive direction (upward for height).
  • Speed = \(|s'(a)|\) is always ________________non-negative.
Example 6
Instantaneous Velocity
Height of a ball: \(s(t) = -16t^2 + 64t + 6\). Find the velocity at \(t = 1\) and \(t = 3\). Interpret each. OpenStax
Find general velocity
\[s'(a) = \lim_{h \to 0}\frac{-32ah - 16h^2 + 64h}{h} = -32a + 64\]
Evaluate and interpret
\[s'(1) = -32 + 64 = \boxed{32 \;\text{ft/sec (moving upward)}}\]
\[s'(3) = -96 + 64 = \boxed{-32 \;\text{ft/sec (moving downward)}}\]
Honors
When Is \(f\) Not Differentiable?
Leibniz notation: \(f'(a)\) is also written \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}\Big|_{x=a}\).
Discontinuity
\(f\) is not continuous at \(a\) — cannot draw tangent.
Corner
Sharp corner — left and right slopes differ (e.g. \(|x|\) at 0).
Cusp / Vertical Tangent
Sharp point or vertical tangent — slope is undefined (\(\to \pm\infty\)).
