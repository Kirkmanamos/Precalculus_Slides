Unit 3 · Exponential & Logarithmic Functions
3.3 Properties of Logarithms
Objective: Use the product, quotient, power, and change-of-base properties to simplify, condense, and expand logarithmic expressions.
product property quotient property power property change of base expand & condense
Reference
Properties of Logarithms
Product Property\(\log_{b}(uv) = \log_{b} u + \log_{b} v\)
Quotient Property\(\log_{b}\!\left(\dfrac{u}{v}\right) = \log_{b} u - \log_{b} v\)
Power Property\(\log_{b}(u^{k}) = k \cdot \log_{b} u\)
Change of Base\(\log_{b} a = \dfrac{\log a}{\log b} = \dfrac{\ln a}{\ln b}\)
Why they work
Logs are exponents, and these are just the exponent rules in disguise. \(b^x \cdot b^y = b^{x+y}\) becomes the product property; \((b^x)^k = b^{xk}\) becomes the power property.
Watch out
Common Mistakes — Things That Look Right But Aren’t
These are NOT log identities
  • \(\log_{a}(uv) \;\ne\; \log_{a} u \cdot \log_{a} v\)
  • \(\log_{a}\!\left(\dfrac{u}{v}\right) \;\ne\; \dfrac{\log_{a} u}{\log_{a} v}\)
  • \(\log_{a}(u + v) \;\ne\; \log_{a} u + \log_{a} v\)
  • \(\log_{a}(u - v) \;\ne\; \log_{a} u - \log_{a} v\)
The fix
The log of a product becomes a sum — not a product of logs. The log of a sum doesn’t simplify at all. The "split" only happens for multiplication and division inside the log.
Examples 1 – 3
Simplify
(1) \(\log_{6} 2 + \log_{6} 3\)   (2) \(\log_{4} 32 - \log_{4} 2\)   (3) \(\log_{5} \sqrt{5}\)
(1) Use the product property
\[\log_{6} 2 + \log_{6} 3 = \log_{6}(2 \cdot 3) = \log_{6} 6 = 1\]
(2) Use the quotient property
\[\log_{4} 32 - \log_{4} 2 = \log_{4}\!\left(\tfrac{32}{2}\right) = \log_{4} 16 = 2\]
(3) Rewrite the radical, then use the power property
\[\log_{5} \sqrt{5} = \log_{5} 5^{1/2} = \tfrac{1}{2} \log_{5} 5 = \tfrac{1}{2}\]
Examples 4 – 5
Condense into a single logarithm
(4) \(\log_{7} a + 2\log_{7} d - \log_{7} 3\)   (5) \(2\log x - \log w - \log z\)
(4) Step 1 — Move coefficients inside (power property)
\[\log_{7} a + \log_{7} d^{2} - \log_{7} 3\]
(4) Step 2 — Combine sums (product) and differences (quotient)
\[\log_{7}\!\left(\dfrac{a\,d^{2}}{3}\right)\]
(5) Step 1 — Move the coefficient
\[\log x^{2} - \log w - \log z\]
(5) Step 2 — Combine (sub becomes division)
\[\log\!\left(\dfrac{x^{2}}{wz}\right)\]
Example 6
Condense \(\tfrac{1}{3}\!\left[\log_{2}(x - 4) - 4\log_{2} x - \log_{2} y\right]\)
Use the properties to write as a single logarithm.
Move the inner coefficient (power property)
\[\tfrac{1}{3}\!\left[\log_{2}(x - 4) - \log_{2} x^{4} - \log_{2} y\right]\]
Combine subtractions inside the bracket
\[\tfrac{1}{3}\,\log_{2}\!\left(\dfrac{x - 4}{x^{4}\,y}\right)\]
Move the \(\tfrac{1}{3}\) inside as an exponent
\[\log_{2}\!\left(\dfrac{x - 4}{x^{4}\,y}\right)^{\!\!1/3}\]
Or write with a radical
\[\log_{2} \sqrt[3]{\dfrac{x - 4}{x^{4}\,y}}\]
Examples 7 – 8
Expand
(7) \(\ln(3x^{2} y^{5})\)   (8) \(\log\!\left(\dfrac{x^{3} y^{2}}{\sqrt{z + 1}}\right)\)
(7) Step 1 — Product → sum
\[\ln 3 + \ln x^{2} + \ln y^{5}\]
(7) Step 2 — Drop exponents (power property)
\[\ln 3 + 2 \ln x + 5 \ln y\]
(8) Step 1 — Rewrite \(\sqrt{z+1}\), split with product/quotient
\[\log x^{3} + \log y^{2} - \log (z + 1)^{1/2}\]
(8) Step 2 — Drop the exponents
\[3\log x + 2\log y - \tfrac{1}{2}\log(z + 1)\]
Examples 9 – 10
Write each in terms of \(\ln 2\) and \(\ln 3\)
(9) \(\ln 6\)   (10) \(\ln \dfrac{2}{27}\)
(9) Step 1 — Factor inside
\[\ln 6 = \ln(2 \cdot 3)\]
(9) Step 2 — Product property
\[\ln 6 = \ln 2 + \ln 3\]
(10) Step 1 — Quotient, then rewrite \(27 = 3^{3}\)
\[\ln \tfrac{2}{27} = \ln 2 - \ln 27 = \ln 2 - \ln 3^{3}\]
(10) Step 2 — Power property
\[\ln \tfrac{2}{27} = \ln 2 - 3 \ln 3\]
Examples 11 – 12
Use the Change-of-Base property (to the nearest thousandth)
(11) \(\log_{7} 8\) using common log   (12) \(\log_{2} 12\) using natural log
(11) Step 1 — Change to base 10
\[\log_{7} 8 = \dfrac{\log 8}{\log 7}\]
(11) Step 2 — Evaluate
\[\log_{7} 8 \approx \dfrac{0.90309}{0.84510} \approx 1.068\]
(12) Step 1 — Change to base \(e\)
\[\log_{2} 12 = \dfrac{\ln 12}{\ln 2}\]
(12) Step 2 — Evaluate
\[\log_{2} 12 \approx \dfrac{2.48491}{0.69315} \approx 3.585\]
