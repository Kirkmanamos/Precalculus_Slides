Unit 1 · Function Analysis
1.6 Operations with Functions
Objective: Add, subtract, multiply, and divide functions, and find compositions of one function with another.
\(f \pm g\) \(f \cdot g\) \(f / g\) \(f \circ g\) domains
Reference
Four arithmetic operations on functions
Addition
\((f + g)(x) = f(x) + g(x)\)
Subtraction
\((f - g)(x) = f(x) - g(x)\)
Multiplication
\((f \cdot g)(x) = f(x)\cdot g(x)\)
Division
\(\displaystyle\left(\tfrac{f}{g}\right)\!(x) = \frac{f(x)}{g(x)},\quad g(x)\neq 0\)
Concept
Domain of a combined function
The rule
The domain of \(f \pm g\), \(f\cdot g\), and \(\tfrac{f}{g}\) is the intersection of the domains of \(f\) and \(g\) — every \(x\) that works for both.
Step 1
Find the domain of \(f\) and the domain of \(g\) separately.
Step 2
Intersect them.
Step 3 · Division only
Also exclude any \(x\) where \(g(x) = 0\).
Example 1
Find \((f - g)(x)\)
Given
\(f(x) = 3x^2 + 7x\) and \(g(x) = 2x^2 - x - 1\)
Set up
\((f - g)(x) = (3x^2 + 7x) - (2x^2 - x - 1)\)
Distribute the minus
\(= 3x^2 + 7x - 2x^2 + x + 1\)
Combine like terms
\(= (3x^2 - 2x^2) + (7x + x) + 1\)
Answer
\((f - g)(x) = x^2 + 8x + 1\)
Example 2
Find \((f\cdot g)(x)\), then evaluate at \(x = 4\)
Given
\(f(x) = 2x - 3\) and \(g(x) = x^2 - 1\)
Set up the product
\((f\cdot g)(x) = (2x - 3)(x^2 - 1)\)
Distribute (FOIL style)
\(= 2x\cdot x^2 - 2x\cdot 1 - 3\cdot x^2 + 3\)
Simplify
\((f\cdot g)(x) = 2x^3 - 3x^2 - 2x + 3\)
Evaluate at \(x = 4\)
\(2(64) - 3(16) - 2(4) + 3 = 128 - 48 - 8 + 3\)
Answer
\((f\cdot g)(4) = 75\)
Example 3
Find \(\left(\tfrac{f}{g}\right)(x)\) and state its domain
Given
\(f(x) = 3x^2 - 2x + 1\) and \(g(x) = x - 4\)
Form the quotient
\(\displaystyle\left(\tfrac{f}{g}\right)(x) = \frac{3x^2 - 2x + 1}{x - 4}\)
Domains separately
\(f\): all reals. \(\;g\): all reals.
Exclude zeros of \(g\)
\(x - 4 = 0 \Rightarrow x = 4\). Since \(4\) makes the denominator \(0\), it is excluded.
Domain
\(\{x \in \mathbb{R} : x \neq 4\}\), or \((-\infty,\,4) \cup (4,\,\infty)\)
Example 4
Find \(\left(\tfrac{g}{f}\right)(x)\) and its domain
Given
\(f(x) = \sqrt{x}\) and \(g(x) = \sqrt{4 - x^2}\)
Form the quotient
\(\displaystyle\left(\tfrac{g}{f}\right)(x) = \frac{\sqrt{4 - x^2}}{\sqrt{x}}\)
Domain of \(f\)
\(\sqrt{x}\) needs \(x \geq 0\). Also \(f(x) = \sqrt{x} = 0\) at \(x = 0\), and \(f\) is in the denominator → exclude \(0\). So \(x > 0\).
Domain of \(g\)
\(\sqrt{4 - x^2}\) needs \(4 - x^2 \geq 0\) → \(-2 \leq x \leq 2\).
Intersect
\((0,\,\infty)\,\cap\,[-2,\,2] = (0,\,2]\)
Domain
\((0,\,2]\)
Concept
Composition of functions
WriteSay
\(f\) composed with \(g\)\((f \circ g)(x) = f(g(x))\)“\(f\) of \(g\) of \(x\)”
\(g\) composed with \(f\)\((g \circ f)(x) = g(f(x))\)“\(g\) of \(f\) of \(x\)”
Not multiplication!
\((f \circ g)(x)\) is not \(f(x)\cdot g(x)\). The small circle \(\circ\) means “plug in.”
How to evaluate
Work from the inside out. Compute the inner function first, then feed its output into the outer.
Order matters
Composition is not commutative. In general \((f\circ g)(x) \neq (g\circ f)(x)\).

Example 5
Evaluate \(f(g(1))\) and \(g(f(2))\)
Given
\(f(x) = 2x + 3\) and \(g(x) = x^2 - 1\)
Part a · Inside first
\(g(1) = (1)^2 - 1 = 0\)
Feed into \(f\)
\(f(g(1)) = f(0) = 2(0) + 3\)
\(f(g(1)) =\)
\(3\)
Part b · Inside first
\(f(2) = 2(2) + 3 = 7\)
Feed into \(g\)
\(g(f(2)) = g(7) = (7)^2 - 1\)
\(g(f(2)) =\)
\(48\)
Example 6
Find \((f\circ g)(x)\) and \((g\circ f)(x)\)
Given
\(f(x) = x - 3\) and \(g(x) = 2x^2 - 1\)
\((f\circ g)(x)\) · plug \(g\) into \(f\)
\(f(g(x)) = f(2x^2 - 1) = (2x^2 - 1) - 3\)
\((f\circ g)(x) =\)
\(2x^2 - 4\)
\((g\circ f)(x)\) · plug \(f\) into \(g\)
\(g(f(x)) = g(x - 3) = 2(x - 3)^2 - 1\)
Expand
\(= 2(x^2 - 6x + 9) - 1 = 2x^2 - 12x + 17\)
Notice they’re different
\((f\circ g)(x) = 2x^2 - 4\) vs. \((g\circ f)(x) = 2x^2 - 12x + 17\)
Example 7
Write each as a composition \(y = f(g(x))\)
Strategy
Identify the inner expression (the part that is wrapped, inside a function). Then the outer is the function being applied to it.
a) \(y = \sqrt{5x - 4}\) · inner
Inner: \(g(x) = 5x - 4\). Outer: \(f(u) = \sqrt{u}\).
a) answer
\(y = f(g(x))\) where \(f(u) = \sqrt{u},\;g(x) = 5x - 4\)
b) \(y = 2^{\cos(x^2)}\) · inner
Innermost: \(h(x) = x^2\). Middle: \(g(u) = \cos(u)\). Outer: \(f(v) = 2^v\).
b) answer
\(y = f(g(h(x)))\) — three-function composition
Review
Operations cheat sheet
Arithmetic
Add, subtract, multiply, divide the outputs. Domain = intersection (and exclude zeros of the denominator for division).
Composition
\((f\circ g)(x) = f(g(x))\). Work inside out. Not multiplication, not commutative.
Domain of \(f \circ g\) requires \(x\) in domain of \(g\) and \(g(x)\) in domain of \(f\).
