Unit 9 · Introduction to Calculus
9.2 Finding Limits: Properties of Limits
Objective: Apply limit laws, use direct substitution, and resolve the indeterminate \(0/0\) form.
limit laws direct substitution \(0/0\) form conjugate method
Reference
Limit Laws
Let \(\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = A\) and \(\lim_{x \to a} g(x) = B\).
Law Formula
Constant\(\lim_{x\to a} k = k\)
Constant Multiple\(\lim_{x\to a}[k \cdot f] = kA\)
Sum / Difference\(\lim_{x\to a}[f \pm g] = A \pm B\)
Product\(\lim_{x\to a}[f \cdot g] = A \cdot B\)
Quotient\(\lim_{x\to a}\frac{f}{g} = \frac{A}{B}\), \(B \ne 0\)
Power / Root\(\lim_{x\to a}[f]^n = A^n\), \(\lim_{x\to a}\sqrt[n]{f} = \sqrt[n]{A}\)
Polynomial Rule
For any polynomial \(p(x)\): \(\;\lim_{x \to a} p(x) =\) ________\(p(a)\) (direct substitution).
Examples 1 & 2
Direct Substitution
Evaluate (a) \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 3}(2x + 5)\) and (b) \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 5}(2x^3 - 3x + 1)\). OpenStax
Part (a)
\[2(3) + 5 = \boxed{11}\]
Part (b)
\[2(5)^3 - 3(5) + 1 = 250 - 15 + 1 = \boxed{236}\]
Example 3
Limit of a Power
Evaluate \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 2}(3x + 1)^5\). OpenStax
Apply power law + substitution
\[[3(2) + 1]^5 = 7^5 = \boxed{16{,}807}\]
Concept

The \(0/0\) Indeterminate Form

If direct substitution gives \(\frac{0}{0}\), the limit may still exist but cannot be evaluated by substitution alone. Simplify first.
1. Factoring
Factor numerator (or denominator), then cancel the common factor creating the \(0/0\).
2. Complex Fractions
Find a common denominator in the numerator, simplify, then cancel \(h\) or \((x - a)\).
3. Conjugate
Multiply top and bottom by the ______________conjugate to eliminate a radical.
Example 4
Factoring Technique
Evaluate \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 2}\dfrac{x^2 - 6x + 8}{x - 2}\). OpenStax
Factor and cancel
\[\frac{x^2 - 6x + 8}{x - 2} = \frac{(x-2)(x-4)}{x-2} = x - 4 \quad (x \ne 2)\]
Take the limit
\[\lim_{x \to 2}(x - 4) = 2 - 4 = \boxed{-2}\]
Example 5
Complex Fraction Technique
Evaluate \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 5}\dfrac{\frac{1}{x} - \frac{1}{5}}{x - 5}\). OpenStax
Combine fractions in numerator
\[\frac{\frac{5 - x}{5x}}{x - 5} = \frac{5 - x}{5x(x - 5)} = \frac{-(x-5)}{5x(x-5)} = \frac{-1}{5x}\]
Take the limit
\[\lim_{x \to 5}\frac{-1}{5x} = \frac{-1}{25} = \boxed{-\frac{1}{25}}\]
Example 6
Conjugate Technique
Evaluate \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 0}\dfrac{\sqrt{25-x} - 5}{x}\). OpenStax
Multiply by conjugate
\[\frac{\sqrt{25-x}-5}{x}\cdot\frac{\sqrt{25-x}+5}{\sqrt{25-x}+5} = \frac{(25-x)-25}{x(\sqrt{25-x}+5)} = \frac{-x}{x(\sqrt{25-x}+5)}\]
Simplify and take limit
\[\frac{-1}{\sqrt{25-x}+5} \;\xrightarrow{x \to 0}\; \frac{-1}{\sqrt{25}+5} = \boxed{-\frac{1}{10}}\]
Example 7
Factoring with a Radical
Evaluate \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 4}\dfrac{4 - x}{\sqrt{x} - 2}\). OpenStax
Factor using difference of squares
\[4 - x = (\sqrt{x})^2 - 4 \;\text{reversed} = -((\sqrt{x})^2 - 4) = -(\sqrt{x}-2)(\sqrt{x}+2)\]
\[\frac{-(\sqrt{x}-2)(\sqrt{x}+2)}{\sqrt{x}-2} = -(\sqrt{x}+2)\]
Take the limit
\[-(\sqrt{4}+2) = -(2+2) = \boxed{-4}\]
Example 8
Limit with Absolute Value
Evaluate \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 7}\dfrac{|x - 7|}{x - 7}\). OpenStax
Check one-sided limits
\[\text{Left } (x < 7): |x-7| = -(x-7) \;\Rightarrow\; \frac{-(x-7)}{x-7} = -1\]
\[\text{Right } (x > 7): |x-7| = x-7 \;\Rightarrow\; \frac{x-7}{x-7} = 1\]
Left \(\ne\) Right \(\Rightarrow\) Limit does not exist.
Review
Can You Now...?
Review these core concepts.
Direct Substitution
For polynomials and well-behaved functions, just plug in \(a\).
Resolve \(0/0\)
Factor, simplify complex fractions, or multiply by a conjugate — then substitute.
One-Sided Check
For absolute value or piecewise, check left and right limits separately.
