Unit 1 · Function Analysis
1.5 Transformations of Functions
Objective: Identify transformations of parent functions from equations and use transformations to sketch graphs.
parent functions translations reflections dilations D.R.S.
Concept
Parent functions you should know
Linear
\(f(x)=x\)
Quadratic
\(f(x)=x^2\)
Cubic
\(f(x)=x^3\)
Absolute value
\(f(x)=|x|\)
Square root
\(f(x)=\sqrt{x}\)
Cube root
\(f(x)=\sqrt[3]{x}\)
Why memorize them?
Every transformed graph in this section starts from one of these parents. Recognize the parent, then apply shifts, reflections, and stretches.
Concept
Three flavors of transformation
Translation
\(g(x) = f(x - h) + k\)
Slide the graph horizontally by \(h\) or vertically by \(k\). Size and shape don’t change.
Reflection
\(-f(x)\) or \(f(-x)\)
Flip across the \(x\)-axis (negate outside) or the \(y\)-axis (negate inside).
Dilation
\(c\cdot f(x)\) or \(f(k x)\)
Stretch or shrink the graph vertically by \(c\) or horizontally by \(1/k\).
Big idea
Changes outside \(f\) affect \(y\) and behave as you’d expect. Changes inside \(f\) affect \(x\) and behave opposite of what they look like.
Reference · Translation
\(g(x) = f(x - h) + k\)
Horizontal
\(f(x - h)\) shifts the graph ________right by \(h\) when \(h>0\).
Vertical
\(f(x) + k\) shifts the graph ________up by \(k\) when \(k>0\).
Watch the sign!
\((x - 3)\) means shift right 3. \((x + 3) = (x - (-3))\) means shift left 3. Read the sign that follows \(x\) and flip it.
Reference · Reflection & Dilation
Outside vs. inside the function
\(-f(x)\) · outside
Reflect over the ________\(x\)-axis. Every \(y\) becomes \(-y\).
\(f(-x)\) · inside
Reflect over the ________\(y\)-axis. Every \(x\) becomes \(-x\).
\(c\cdot f(x)\) · vertical
\(|c|>1\): stretch away from \(x\)-axis. \(0<|c|<1\): shrink toward \(x\)-axis.
\(f(k x)\) · horizontal
\(|k|>1\): shrink toward \(y\)-axis. \(0<|k|<1\): stretch away from \(y\)-axis.
Example 1
Describe the transformation from the parent
1) \(g(x) = |x - 1| + 12\)
parent: \(|x|\)
  • \((x - 1)\) → shift right 1
  • \(+\,12\) → shift up 12
2) \(h(x) = -\sqrt{x + 8}\)
parent: \(\sqrt{x}\)
  • \((x + 8)\) → shift left 8
  • leading \(-\) → reflect over \(x\)-axis
3) \(f(x) = 3(x - 7)^3\)
parent: \(x^3\)
  • \((x - 7)\) → shift right 7
  • coefficient \(3\) → vertical stretch by 3
4) \(h(x) = \sqrt[3]{2x} - 9\)
parent: \(\sqrt[3]{x}\)
  • inside \(2x\) → horizontal shrink by \(\tfrac{1}{2}\)
  • \(-\,9\) → shift down 9
Tip
Order matters — use D.R.S.
When does order matter?
If a function has more than one transformation in the same direction (horizontal or vertical), the order you apply them matters. A shift must happen after a reflection or dilation in that direction.
D · R · S
D · Dilate
Apply stretches and shrinks first.
R · Reflect
Then flip across axes.
S · Shift
Translate last, after everything else.
Dilate → Reflect → Shift. Always in that order.
Concept
Vertical stretch vs. horizontal shrink
They can look the same!
Some graphs could be described as a vertical stretch or a horizontal shrink. Both stretch the same-looking curve — you pick based on the equation.
\(y = 4x^2\) · vertical stretch by 4
\(y = (2x)^2\) · horizontal shrink by \(\tfrac{1}{2}\)
Key fact
\((2x)^2 = 4x^2\). The graphs are identical. Either description is valid, but the equation tells you which.
Rule of thumb
Multiplier outside \(f\) → vertical. Multiplier inside \(f\) → horizontal.

Review
Transformation cheat sheet
Recognize parent
Before transforming, identify the parent: \(x\), \(x^2\), \(x^3\), \(|x|\), \(\sqrt{x}\), \(\sqrt[3]{x}\).
Outside vs. inside
Outside \(f\) affects \(y\) (does what you expect). Inside \(f\) affects \(x\) (does the opposite).
Apply in order: Dilate → Reflect → Shift.
